Chennai

Chennai (formerly known as Madras) is the capital of the state of Tamil Nadu and is India's fourth largest city. It is located on the Coromandel Coast of the Bay of Bengal. With an estimated population of 8.9 million (2014), the 400-year-old city is the 31st largest metropolitan area in the world. Chennai boasts of a rich history that dates back to around 400 years ago when it was a tiny fishing village called Madraspatnam. The city was developed by the British East India Company as a major port and naval base. Around 20th century, Chennai became an important administrative headquarter and the capital of Madras Presidency.
